Keyboard Race - Spring 2020

A game created at the DIS Game Jam 2020 over a period of 24 hours. The theme of the jam was "One Button". The game is designed around each player having one button that allows them to jump over obstacles. Each player's button changes after they press it, causing them to have to wrestle and fight for buttons around the keyboard. Game is available here! GitHub Repository

Global Health Research Paper - Spring 2018

A paper written after conducting research and field study in Cuba, Costa Rica, and Denmark. The paper analyzes the influence of out-of-pocket payments on healthcare systems. The research process included visits to hospitals, primary care clinics, and rural communities. Interviews were conducted with medical practictioners and an assortment of patients with various backgrounds and identities.

ShelfR - Fall 2018

An application built to give people a simple way to keep track of their food purchases and the expiration status of their food. Given a product a user might wish to buy, the app searches for the product's expiration date and sets up a calendar/notification to alert users if they haven't used up their food as the expiration date gets closer. After the user has trained the app with sufficient data based on their average rate of consumption of certain products, the app is also designed to make intelligent suggestions on how much of a certain product a user should buy to avoid over purchasing. It was developed during over a 48 hour period during the ISU Hackathon.
